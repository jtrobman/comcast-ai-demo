Comcast Business network-management guidance explains that actual Internet speed can depend on many factors outside Comcast's direct control, including customer device performance, the type of connection between the device and router or gateway, congestion or high usage at the destination, and modem or gateway capability. Comcast Business notes that in-office WiFi connections between a device and router or gateway can be slower than wired connections.

The same source says modem or gateway performance can degrade over time and that some devices may not support higher speed tiers. It also encourages customers to contact customer service if they have concerns about modem or gateway performance or speed capabilities.

Comcast Business says customers can test speeds using a Comcast Business web-based speed test, including tests to the cable modem gateway and from a customer device on the local network. Other public speed tests can be useful but should be treated as guides rather than definitive measurements of Comcast network performance.

For WiFi hotspots, Comcast Business explains that performance can vary based on other users, device factors, distance from the hotspot, attenuation from walls or foliage, and interference from devices using the same unlicensed spectrum.

Demo use: This source supports technician-facing context about WiFi versus wired symptoms, gateway health, speed-test limitations, and avoiding overconfident claims about root cause without telemetry.
